The problem

Dating apps were never going to replace face to face connection.

Three things stand in the way.

Approaching is becoming a lost art
People are making spontaneous real-life connections less than ever compared to decades of the past.
Without a signal, why would you?
If you don't know whether someone's interested, walking up to them feels like a risk nobody wants to take. The fear of rejection wins every time.
No real-world bridge
Apps stop at the match. Nobody designed the moment that takes you from the screen to the same room.
How it works

Chemistry first.
Location closes the gap.

Brush works in four steps, each one building toward a real moment.

👤
Match on chemistry
Browse profiles of people who frequent the same places as you. Likes are blind, nobody knows until it's mutual.
💬
Chat with a purpose
A short, intentional chat opens. Not endless messaging, just enough to know the vibe is real.
🔁
Flip the switch
When you're both ready, each of you toggles open to meeting in person. A mutual agreement with no pressure.
